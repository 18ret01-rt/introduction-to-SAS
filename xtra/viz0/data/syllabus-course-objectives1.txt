### Student Learning Objectives

At the end of this course, students will be able to:

1. Produce scatterplots, bar charts, and line graphs using R (substitute SAS or SPSS) software.

2. Change default features of the graphs, including color, size, and shape.

3. Describe the components of color and how colors are used in visualizations.

4. Apply the psychological theories of perception to influence how others view and interpret your graphs.

5. List the advantages and disadvantages of different types of graphs.

6. Use the skills you have learned to develop an effective visualization of an independent data set.
